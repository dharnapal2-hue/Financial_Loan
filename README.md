<h1> Bank Loan Report </h1>
The Bank Loan Report in Power BI highlights a strong overall portfolio, with 85.53% of loans fully paid and only 12.73% charged off, though defaults remain a risk to monitor. The customer base is largely composed of renters (47.04%) and mortgage holders (44.58%), while owners represent just 8.06%, showing that those without outright ownership are the primary borrowers. Financial metrics reveal $139.70M in loans issued and $150.91M in payments collected, supported by an average interest rate of 11.2%. The Loan Amount by Month line chart shows clear seasonality, with borrowing peaking in December at $17M and dipping to $9M in August, suggesting year-end demand. Meanwhile, the Loan Amount by Grades bar chart demonstrates a conservative lending strategy, with most loans concentrated in safer grades A and B, while exposure to riskier grades F and G is minimal. Overall, the Power BI visuals provide a clear narrative of strong repayment behavior, seasonal borrowing trends, and prudent risk management.
